    Robert Rinder parents-British criminal barrister, Robert Michael Rinder was born on May 31, 1978, in Westminster, London in the United Kingdom.

    Rinder was born to Angela Cohen. He was raised in Southgate and had his education at the National Youth Theatre, Queen Elizabeth’s School, Osidge Primary School, and Barnet.

    Eventually, Rinder gave up acting because Benedict Cumberbatch, a peer and eventually a close friend, “was so good.” Rinder graduated with first-class honors from the University of Manchester after majoring in politics and contemporary history.

    Robert Rinder career

    After graduating from the University of Manchester in 2001, Rinder was admitted to the bar and began his pupillage at 2 Paper Buildings. Then he moved into 2 Hare Court as a renter.

    He then became an expert in instances involving global fraud, money laundering, and other financial crimes.

    Following the killings of Leticia Shakespeare and Charlene Ellis in January 2003, he was involved in prosecutions. He also defended British troops accused of manslaughter following the deaths of prisoners in Iraq.

    In the British Overseas Territory of the Turks & Caicos Islands, he has been involved in the investigation and prosecution of suspected bribery, corruption, and fraud since 2010.

    Rinder created television screenplays in his spare time while working as an attorney. He ran across producer Tom McLennan while trying to sell one.

    His plan for a revival of the 1970s television show Crown Court was turned down by ITV in favor of a British Judge Judy, and McLennan gave Rinder the chance to host it.

    He served as the titular judge in the reality courtroom series Judge Rinder from 2014 until 2020. He has been compared to a British Judge Judy.

    A book titled Rinder Rules was published by Rinder in 2015. Judge Rinder’s Crime Stories, a new series he debuted in 2016, featured reconstructions of actual events.

    Additionally, he was the host of the BBC Radio 5 Live program Raising the Bar, which he began by speaking with the late Lord Chief Justice, Lord Judge. After a 30-year break, Rinder revived Crown Court on ITV in December 2017 under the new moniker Judge Rinder’s Crown Court.

    In December 2018, Rinder hosted Good Year Bad Year, a one-off show on Channel 4 where Rinder and a variety of famous guests reviewed the hot-button highs and lows of 2018. He started presenting The Rob Rinder Verdict on Channel 4 in 2019.

    Alongside Susanna Reid, Rinder made an appearance on Celebrity Gogglebox’s second season in July 2020. My Family, the Holocaust and Me, a documentary by Rinder that was shown on BBC One in November 2020, assisted Jewish families in learning the real truth about what actually happened to their ancestors during the Holocaust. The documentary was hailed as “a vital history lesson” and got considerable praise.

    In 2022, Rinder has been a presenter on ITV’s Good Morning Britain. The 10-part crime series Rob Rinder’s Interrogation Secrets, hosted by Rinder, was ordered by Crime & Investigation UK on July 14, 2022. It debuted on September 4, 2022.

    The next co-host of Amazing Hotels: Life Beyond The Lobby will be chef Monica Galetti and Rob Rinder, it was revealed in November 2022.

    The Holy Land and Us: Our Untold Experiences, a two-part documentary that explores the individualized experiences of families of Jewish and Palestinian ancestry, was published by Rinder in March 2023 for BBC Two.

    It was revealed in August 2023 that Rinder will co-present The Lost Princes (w/t), a future Channel 4 history program, alongside Philippa Langley.

    Who are Robert Rinder’s parents?

    Robert Rinder was born to Angela Cohen and her then-husband. He has an older brother named Craig Rinder. His mother is the chair of the 45 Aid Society.
