    Amy Schumer’s Biography

    Amy Beth Schumer born June 1, 1981, is an American stand-up comedian, actress, writer, producer, and director who ventured into comedy in the early 2000s before appearing as a contestant on the fifth season of the NBC reality competition series Last Comic Standing in 2007.

    From 2013 to 2016, Amy Schumer was the creator, co-producer, co-writer, and star of the Comedy Central sketch comedy series Inside Amy Schumer, for which she received a Peabody Award and was nominated for five Primetime Emmy Awards, winning Outstanding Variety Sketch Series in 2015.

    Amy Schumer wrote and made her film debut in a starring role in Trainwreck (2015), for which she received nominations for the Writers Guild of America Award for Best Original Screenplay and the Golden Globe Award for Best Actress Motion Picture Comedy or Musical. She published a memoir in 2016, The Girl with the Lower Back Tattoo, which held the top position on The New York Times Non-Fiction Best Seller list for two weeks.

    In the same year 2016, Amy Schumer was nominated for two Grammy Awards: for Best Comedy Album for Amy Schumer: Live at the Apollo, and Best Spoken Word Album for The Girl with the Lower Back Tattoo. In 2018, she starred in the comedy film I Feel Pretty and garnered a Tony Award for Best Actress in a Play nomination for her Broadway debut in Meteor Shower.

    Amy Schumer was born in the Upper East Side of Manhattan, New York City, New York, to Sandra Jane and Gordon David Schumer, who owned a baby furniture company. Amy Schumer’s father was born into a Jewish family of Ukrainian origin. She is a second cousin to Chuck Schumer, a US Senator and Senate Majority Leader. Her mother was of Protestant origin, with deep New England roots, and converted to Judaism before her marriage. Amy Schumer was raised Jewish and said she suffered from anti-Semitism as a child.

    The Schumer family was wealthy in the early years, thanks to the success of her father’s Manhattan furniture company. When she was 9, her father’s business failed and went bankrupt, and at that time, or when she was 12, her father was diagnosed with multiple sclerosis and after some time, her parents divorced.

    Amy Schumer moved with her mother to Long Island, where she lived in Rockville Center, New York, where she attended Southside High School. After graduating in 1999, she was voted for both “Class Clown” and “Teacher’s Worst Nightmare”. She attended the Hebrew school of the Central Synagogue of Nassau County, a Reform synagogue in Rockville Centre, where her mother served on the board.

    After high school, Amy Schumer moved to nearby Baltimore, Maryland while attending Towson College. In 2003 she completed her theater studies. After her college graduation, she returned to New York City, where she studied at the William Esper Studio for two years, where she worked as a bartender and waitress. She also temporarily moved to Santa Barbara, California (with a boyfriend she described as abusive), where she worked as a pedicab driver

    She has her sister Kim Caramel, a comedy writer and producer, and her half-brother Jason Stein, a musician in Chicago, Illinois. Amy Schumer dated professional wrestler Nick Nemeth, better known by his ring name Dolph Ziggler, and comedian Anthony Jeselnik.

    She married chef and farmer Chris Fisher on February 13, 2018, in Malibu, California. She announced her first child pregnancy in October 2018. On May 5, 2019, Amy Schumer gave birth to a boy, Jean. Amy Schumer originally gave Gene his middle name Attell, in honor of comedian Dave Attell, but she realized that “Gene Attell” sounded like “genitals” and changed it to David. Her son was born by cesarean section because of endometriosis. In September 2021, Amy Schumer’s uterus was removed to alleviate symptoms associated with the disease.

    Amy Schumer’s Career

    After graduating with a degree in theater from Towson University in 2003 and moving to New York City, Amy Schumer portrayed a young woman diagnosed with breast cancer in the Off-Off-Broadway black comedy Keeping Abreast.

    She started doing stand-up comedy on June 1, 2004, when she first performed at Gotham Comedy Club. In 2007, she recorded a Live at Gotham episode for Comedy Central before appearing on Last Comic Standing; she later recalled that she thought of the episode as her “big break”.

    Amy Schumer had a supporting role on the Comedy Central reality show Reality Bites in 2008. In 2009, she starred in an advertising campaign for Butterfinger. Amy Schumer was a regular guest on the late-night show Red Eye from 2007 to 2012 alongside Fox News’ Greg Gutfeld. Her first Comedy Central Presents special aired on April 2, 2010. She co-hosted “A Different Spin” with Mark Hoppus in 2011, later titled “Hoppus on Music”. She has also contributed to Cosmopolitan.

    Amy Schumer acted in three films in 2012: the independent comedy Price Check, the comedy-drama Seeking a Friend for the End of the World, and the independent comedy Sleepwalk with Me. Amy Schumer also appeared on The Comedy Central Roast of Charlie Sheen in September 2011, and The Comedy Central Roast of Roseanne Barr in August 2012.

    In June 2012, Amy Schumer began working on a sketch comedy series for Comedy Central. The show included single-camera vignettes in which Schumer performed an “evolved version” of himself. The vignette is linked to footage of Schumer’s stand-up performance.

    The show, Inside Amy Schumer, premiered on Comedy Central on April 30, 2013. Inside Amy Schumer was selected for the second season starting in 2014. Her behind-the-scenes miniseries titled “Behind Amy Schumer” premiered in 2012. The third season premiered on April 21, 2015, and the fourth season was ordered on the same day.

    Amy Schumer made her Broadway debut in 2017 with Steve Martin’s comedy play Meteor Shower, about two couples in 1993 who spend an evening with each other. Her performance received acclaim and garnered her a nomination for the Tony Award for Best Actress in a Play. In 2018, she starred in the comedy film I Feel Pretty.

    Amy Schumer starred in the 2021 film The Humans, based on the play of the same name by Stephen Cullum. She wrote, directed, and starred in Hulu’s 10-episode comedy series Life & Beth, which premiered on March 18, 2022. The return of the sketch series Inside Amy Schumer, which had been dormant since 2016, was announced in February 2021, with five more specials to be released via Paramount+.

    In January 2022, Amy Schumer made a guest appearance in the second season of Only Murders in the Building. On March 27, 2022, she co-hosted the 94th Academy Awards with Regina Hall and Wanda Sykes. In June, she joined the cast of Jerry Seinfeld’s Unfrosted: The Story of a Poptart.
