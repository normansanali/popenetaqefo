    Biography

    KJ Apa whose real name is Keneti James Fitzgerald Apa, is an award winning New Zealand actor born on June 17, 1997.

    Apa was born in Auckland to Keneti and Tessa Apa. His father is Samoan and a matai (chief) of his village in Samoa while his mother is European New Zealander.

    He also has two siblings (older sisters) and he is the nephew of former rugby union player and coach Michael Jones.

    KJ also went to King’s College in Auckland.

    Career

    Apa started his career with a role as Kane Jenkins in the New Zealand primetime soap opera “Shortland Street” from 2013 to 2015.

    He came to limelight for his role in the CW drama series “Riverdale”.

    He has starred in other movies such as; A Dog’s Purpose, I Still Believe, GThe Last Summer and so on.
