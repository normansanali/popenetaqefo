    Who is Tara Strong? 'Loki' fans laud Miss Minutes' 'perfect' voice casting

    Marvel Studio's finally dropped Episode 1 of its eagerly awaited show 'Loki', with Tom Hiddleston returning as the God of Mischief. Loki also features Owen Wilson, Gugu Mbatha-Raw, Richard E.     04 Sep 2024, 00:00

    Marvel Studio's finally dropped Episode 1 of its eagerly awaited show 'Loki', with Tom Hiddleston returning as the God of Mischief. Loki also features Owen Wilson, Gugu Mbatha-Raw, Richard E. Grant, and  Sophia Di Martino. Now, we've learned that another popular actress has joined the cast as 'Miss Minutes'.

    The show follows Loki's adventures with the Time Variance Authority (TVA), a bureaucratic institution whose role is to ensure the "proper flow of time" in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). The show has spawned a large number of fan theories, most notably the character of Di Martino. Marvel hasn't announced her role, leaving many fans to speculate she could be playing Lady Loki.

    Episode 1 ends with Mobius (Owen Wilson) telling Loki he needs his help to catch a 'variant' which could be Di Martino's character as many have speculated. With fans enjoying Easter eggs along the way, one particular egg that they seemed to love was Miss Minutes, the mascot of the TVA.

    Who plays Miss Minutes?

    While not a prominent role, Miss Minutes is bound to be a recurring character in the show. In Episode 1, she brings a very confused Loki up to speed about where he is and why he is at the TVA. The hilarious scene got many people talking, mostly about the voice of Miss Minutes. There was no formal announcement about who would play the lovable orange clock, but Marvel did spill the beans in the credits of the show. 

    Miss Minutes is voiced by Canadian actress Tara Strong, who is well known for her voice work. Strong has voiced Harley Quinn, Barbara Gordon, and Poison Ivy in numerous DC video games and animations. She's also cast as Raven on 'Teen Titans GO!' Strong is also no stranger to Marvel, having voiced Mary Jane Watson and the Scarlet Witch in some cartoons in the past. Strong's voice work goes back to the late 1980s, and she's been involved in some pretty popular projects. These include 'X-Men', 'The Powerpuff Girls', 'Family Guy', 'Samurai Jack', and numerous 'Ben 10' shows. She's also appeared in films like 'Star Wars: The Rise of Skywalker', 'The Secret Life of Pets', 'Ted', 'Bolt', and 'Ice Age'.
