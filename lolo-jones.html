    Professional Life

    Professionally, Lolo Jones is a famous hurdler and bobsledder. Lolo specializes in the 60-meter and 100-meter hurdles.

    The sportsperson won three NCAA titles and also gained 11 All-American honors. She made this achievement while at Louisiana State University.

    Moreover, Lolo won indoor national titles in the years 2007, 2008, and 2009 in the 60-meter hurdles. Lolo won gold medals at the World Indoor Championship in 2008 and 2010

    . In bobsled, Lolo won the 2021 IBSF World Championships as the brave woman for Kaillie Humphries.

    Caption: Lolo and Kaillie Humphries posing for a photo with their trophy. Source: Instagram

    Additionally, Lolo was favored to win the 100-meter hurdles at the 2008 Beijing Olympics. However, she tripped on the penultimate hurdle and hence finished in seventh place.

    The sportsperson went on to win gold at the 2008 World Athletics Final. During this tournament, Lolo beat the newly crowned Olympic champion, Dawn Harper, with a time of 12.56.

    More Details

    Further, Jones was the American record holder in the 60-meter hurdles with a time of 7.72. She held this record until the year 2018 when both Kendra Harrison and Sharika Nelvis improved the time to 7.70.

    Moreover, Lolo is also a part of the competing brake women on the U.S. national bobsled team. In this sport, Lolo and her team won a gold medal at the 2013 World Championships.

    Jones also represented the United States at the 2014 Winter Olympics. This made Lolo one of the few athletes competing in both the Summer and Winter Olympic Games.

    Further, Lolo was a track star at LSU in college. Her team also received the crown of SEC champion 6 times. Also, Lolo finished 1st in the World Indoor Championships 60-meter hurdles.

    This led to the speculation that Lolo was the favorite in the 100-meter hurdles at the 2012 London games.
