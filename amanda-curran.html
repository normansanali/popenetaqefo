    Career and Professional Life

    She was able to join WSFA 12 News in July 2014 as a meteorologist. Likewise, while attending Lyndon State College,

    she was able to work for the Vermont Center for Community Journalism (VCCJ-News 7). It is a student-run news station broadcasting to cities and towns in northeastern Vermont.

    She prepared forecasts for the station’s Emmy Award-winning 3 pm and 5:30 pm, and online newscasts during her time at the VCCJ. She even was able to spend two summers interning at her local television station, WMUR-TV.

    Moreover, Curran was the Best Weather Anchor of the Year for 2017 by the Associated Press and in the year 2015, she was able to receive first runner-up in the same category.

    Recently, she has been able to earn the American Meteorological Society’s Certified Broadcast Meteorologist (CBM) designation, professional recognition of the quality of her weather broadcasts.
