    The top 5 Earliest Stoppages in Boxing History

    Boxing has seen its fair share of quick and decisive fights, with some matches ending in the blink of an eye. Here are the top 5 earliest stoppages in boxing history that have left lasting impressions on the sport.

    09 Aug 2024, 00:00

    Boxing has seen its fair share of quick and decisive fights, with some matches ending in the blink of an eye. Here are the top 5 earliest stoppages in boxing history that have left lasting impressions on the sport.

    1. Mike Tyson vs. Marvis Frazier (July 26, 1986)

    In a bout that lasted a mere 30 seconds, Mike Tyson made quick work of Marvis Frazier, son of former heavyweight champion Joe Frazier. Tyson unleashed a flurry of punches, backing Frazier into a corner before a devastating uppercut sent him crashing to the canvas. The referee immediately called an end to the fight, solidifying Tyson's reputation as a fearsome knockout artist.

    2. Gerald McClellan vs. Jay Bell (November 6, 1993)

    Gerald McClellan wasted no time in dispatching Jay Bell in their middleweight clash, which lasted just 20 seconds. McClellan landed a thunderous right hand that dropped Bell, who was unable to beat the count. This lightning-fast stoppage remains one of the quickest in boxing history, showcasing McClellan's explosive power.

    3. Daniel Jimenez vs. Harald Geier (March 26, 1994)

    In a super bantamweight title fight, Daniel Jimenez delivered a stunning performance by halting Harald Geier in a mere 17 seconds. Jimenez unleashed a barrage of punches, overwhelming Geier and forcing the referee to step in and wave off the fight, making it one of the shortest world title fights ever.

    4. Pongsaklek Wonjongkam vs. Malcolm Tunacao (March 19, 1999)

    Pongsaklek Wonjongkam set the record for the fastest world title fight stoppage when he faced Malcolm Tunacao for the WBC flyweight title. Wonjongkam wasted no time, launching a fierce assault that culminated in a devastating knockout just 17 seconds into the first round, leaving Tunacao unable to continue.

    5. James Thunder vs. Crawford Grimsley (May 30, 1997)

    James Thunder's thunderous power was on full display when he collided with Crawford Grimsley in a heavyweight bout. In a blink-and-you-miss-it moment, Thunder connected with a colossal right hand that instantly floored Grimsley, prompting the referee to call a halt to the fight after just 13 seconds, cementing Thunder's reputation as a heavyweight with immense punching power.

    These earliest stoppages in boxing history stand as testament to the raw power and skill possessed by these fighters. In the blink of an eye, these bouts were over, leaving an indelible mark on the sport and showcasing the electrifying and unpredictable nature of boxing.
